Real Ginger Ale

<

Ingredients

  • Fresh Ginger
  • Limes
  • White or Brown Sugar
  • Drinking Water
  • Carbonated Water

Steps

  1. Remove skin from ginger by sliding across it with a spoon (alternative: use a carrot peeler). Then dice the ginger.
  2. Add the diced ginger into a saucepan. For every cup of ginger, we will add two cups of water. For a decent amount of ginger ale to consume for the week, use two cups of ginger.
  3. Grate some of the lime so that the shavings fall into the saucepan.
  4. Heat the mixture to boil. Once it is boiling, turn the heat down and place a cover over the pan. Wait 15 minutes.
  5. Run the mixture through a strainer, and collect the juices that fall from the strainer. Push down on the solid matter in the strainer with a spoon or a bowl to get as much juice out as possible.
  6. Add the juice back into the pan. For each cup of ginger, add a cup of sugar.
  7. Heat the liquid back to a simmer. Let it cool for 15 minutes, stirring regularly.
  8. Afterwards, place the liquid in a bowl, and let it cool for an hour.
  9. If storing, place the syrup in an airtight container. It should last like this for about 2 weeks before going bad.
  10. If serving, mix the syrup with carbonated water. Optinally, you can add lime juice to change the flavor a bit.
